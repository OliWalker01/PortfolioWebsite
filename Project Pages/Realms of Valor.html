OpenCV AR Game - Realms of Valor

During my final year university module, Advanced Computer Games Development, I was required to develop a VR/AR game using an engine of my choice. I opted to use Unreal Engine 4 while also implementing the OpenCV Library.
The outcome of this module was 'Realms of Valor' which uses a range of features from OpenCV such as colour detection and hand recognition, allowing the player to control the game using various coloured objects as well as their hand.
Realms of Valor is a turn-based fighting game where the player takes the role of a Warrior who must combat goblins growing stronger and stronger as they progress.
Combat is controlled through the player holding up coloured objects, the game has been optimised to work with Uno cards, for example, with Red being assigned to an attack move and Green being assigned to heal.
Another one of the OpenCV features which has been implemented is hand recognition, after each battle the player earns Skill Points based on their performance, these points can be exchanged for upgrades allowing the player to take on higher-ranked enemies. These upgrades are chosen by the player by holding up a certain number of fingers corresponding to the upgrade they want to perform.
