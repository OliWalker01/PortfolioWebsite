Incident: Gamma

During my final year at the University of Huddersfield, as part of my group project module, I had the pleasure of working with a small group of very talented artists, designers and programmers to produce a small Video Game.
The outcome of this module was 'Incident: Gamma' which was developed using Unreal Engine 5, and loosely based on the events of Chornobyl, where you the player, takes control of a liquidator set with the task of exploring a nuclear reactor to find the source of a radiation leak.
During this project, I was tasked with developing and implementing various features, one of which i arguably the most influential elements of the entire game, the radiation system. Coding a function that decides how much radiation the player takes on depending on how close they are to the source and how strong it is.
Further development of the game is planned, with plans to grow the storyline of the game to create an impactful tale. As well as further advancements in the radiation system to ensure it is as realistic as possible.
